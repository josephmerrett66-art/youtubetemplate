03
Editing Structure
0:00 – 0:10
Hook
Pull them in immediately.
Fast cuts mixing 5D and Z6. No intro, no context yet — just texture and movement. Drop them into the moment.
Clean shot → messy clip → voice line
0:10 – 0:30
Context
Talking head — what's happening.
5D only. Stable frame. Say what the video is about. No fluff, no intro music, no "welcome back".
0:30 – 2:30
Process
The actual work, unpolished.
Alternate cinematic 5D shots with raw Z6 clips. Let the contrast breathe. Every time the pace dulls, cut to Z6.
Getting boring? Add a Z6 clip.
2:30 → end
Reflection
Be honest about what happened.
What worked, what didn't. This is the part people remember. No forced positivity, no call to action monologue.
04
Style Rules
5D Mark II
Clean.
Still.
Cinematic.
  • Sharp focus, stable frame
  • Deliberate composition every shot
  • Slow, intentional moves only
  • Never handheld unless chosen
Konica Minolta Z6
Raw.
Loose.
Alive.
  • Handheld — imperfect is the point
  • Slight chaos is texture, not failure
  • Don't grade it to look like the 5D
  • It should feel like behind the scenes
Never blur this line — the contrast between cameras is the visual language of the channel.
